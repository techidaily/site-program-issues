## Fix 1: Check your network

 The Origin download requires a stable network. If you’re seeing slow speeds, it’s most likely an issue with your network. In this case, you can:

 1)**Restart your router** .

 Unplug your router and after one minute, plug it back in and make it reconnect.

 2)**Temporarily disconnect other devices** .

 With so many devices connected to your network, Origin download can be quite slow. So if possible, disconnect them while downloading.

## Fix 5: Start Origin in R&D mode

 1) Right-click on the empty space of your desktop and select**New** \>**Text Document** to create a new empty document.

![Use R&D Mode Origin; Start Origin in R&D Mode](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/Start-Origin-in-RD-Mode-1.png)

 2) Double-click on the**New Text Document** you just created.

 3) Paste the lines into the document:

 **\[connection\]**
 **EnvironmentName=production**

 **\[Feature\]**
 **CdnOverride=akamai**

![slow download speed](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/RD-mode.png)

 4) Click**File** \>**Save As** .

 When things don’t work right with your Origin download, clearing your cache can help fix the issue. But before you clear the cache, make sure the Origin app is fully closed.

 First, in the**menu bar** , click**Origin** and then**Exit** .

![exit Origin](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/exit-Origin.png)

 1) Press the**Windows logo key** +**R** together on your keyboard to open the Run box.

 2) Type**taskmgr.exe** and click**OK** .

![open task manager](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/task-manager.png)

 3) Under the**Processes** tab, make sure that**Origin .exe** and**OriginWebHelperService.exe** aren’t on there. (They’re listed in alphabetical order.) If they’re, right-click on it and click**End task** .

 1) Press**Windows logo key** +**R** together on your keyboard.

 2) Type**%ProgramData%/Origin** and click**OK** .

![open the cache files](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/open-the-cache-files.png)
